Milestones in Computer History

A journey through the technological breakthroughs that shaped modern computing

Hardware & Architecture
1947
The Transistor

Revolutionized electronics by replacing vacuum tubes, enabling smaller and more efficient devices.

Additional Information

Invented at Bell Labs by John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley. The transistor is the fundamental building block of modern electronic devices.

  • Replaced bulky vacuum tubes
  • Enabled the development of smaller computers
  • Reduced power consumption significantly
  • Paved the way for integrated circuits
Performance Impact

Transistors were smaller, more reliable, and used less power than vacuum tubes. They enabled the development of smaller, faster, and more efficient computers.
